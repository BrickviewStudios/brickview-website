Virtual Reality

“VR is a way to escape the real world into something more fantastic. It has the potential to be the most social technology of all time”
- Palmer Luckey, Founder of Oculus Rift
What is VR?
Ever wondered what would it be like if you ever got chance to experience something that doesn’t exist, like experiencing the world as it was 200 years back ,walking on Mars or even living in the world of your favorite superheroes?

Well, with the best of Virtual Reality you might surely be able to do all these things- and many more –even without leaving your home!

Virtual Reality means creating a simulation of a real world experience that a person can enter and leave at any time using technology.

It is a believable, interactive 3D computer-created world such that the user suspends belief and accepts it as a real environment.

A person can then use a variety of electronic equipment to interact with and control the environment. Current VR technology most commonly uses VR headsets , sometimes in combination with physical environments or props, to generate realistic images, sounds and other sensations that simulate a user's physical presence in a virtual or imaginary environment.

These are also known as haptic systems.

Virtual Reality finds its application in entertainment, gaming, education, marketing, architectural and urban design,so on and so forth.

Although this technology has not been utilised to it’s fullest due to the expensive hardware requirements,the future of VR is going to be a bright one.

Research firm IDC predicts that the virtual and augmented reality market will dramatically expand from just over $9 billion last year to a whopping $215 billion by 2021! That incredible 118% compound annual growth rate in four years would make VR one of the fastest-growing industries on the planet, which growth-minded investors simply cannot ignore.

Tech Giants like HTC, Facebook and Sony are creating their own hardware at reasonable prices. With the HTC Vive, Facebook's Oculus Rift and Sony's PlayStation VR trending in the market- its not going to be too long before VR will change the way we see things.


Augmented Reality

The “a real-time view” of a physical environment “enhanced” by adding “computer-generated digital information” to that environment is augmented reality. The purpose of Augmented Reality is to redefine or augment how an individual would interact with and interpret the real world by introducing virtual information to his or her direct surroundings and approach to the real world environment as a whole. It is in stark contrast to Virtual Reality where, in the case of the latter, the user is fully immersed in a synthetic and artificial environment in which the user is incapable of a vivid and direct vision of the real world. Augmented Reality has the capacity to augment vision, hearing and, to a lesser extent, taste, touch and smell. It also has the potential to substitute a sense which an individual may lack. For example, a blind user could manage a computer through the use of artificial speech. There are many different types of Augmented Reality. For example, Mediated Reality, where physical objects are replaced or covered by virtual objects that match the background, is also considered to be a form of Augmented Reality.

Augmented Reality is typically employed in various fields, such as entertainment, medicine, education and the future of augmented reality.

While we think that AR is still not in daily use and is an advance technology only used by professionals,By the end of 2018, we expect about 800 million smartphones to have both an OS with dedicated AR support. 2018 is likely to be a year of progress and experimentation for AR. The quantity of premium AR devices will swell. There will be tens of thousands of AR apps.
